Seated & Lying Meditation

Sukhasana Meditation (Easy Pose)

Comfortable seated meditation for calm awareness.

Sukhasana Meditation (Easy Pose)

Sukhasana Meditation is a simple seated posture that allows the body to relax while keeping the spine upright. It is ideal for beginners who want to develop focus and awareness. This posture encourages steady breathing and mental clarity. Sitting comfortably helps reduce physical distractions during meditation. Regular practice calms the nervous system and improves concentration. It is suitable for both short and long meditation sessions.

Benefits
  • Improves focus
  • Encourages calm breathing
  • Reduces mental stress
  • Comfortable for beginners
  • Supports upright posture

Padmasana Meditation (Lotus Pose)

Traditional posture for deep meditation.

Padmasana Meditation (Lotus Pose)

Padmasana Meditation is a classical seated posture used for deep meditation practices. It creates a stable base that supports an upright spine. This posture promotes physical stillness and mental focus. It helps regulate breathing and energy flow. Regular practice increases body awareness and concentration. It is recommended for practitioners with good hip flexibility.

Benefits
  • Enhances concentration
  • Promotes stability
  • Improves posture
  • Supports deep meditation
  • Balances energy flow

02

Shavasana Meditation (Corpse Pose)

Deep relaxation through lying meditation.

Shavasana Meditation (Corpse Pose)

Shavasana Meditation is practiced by lying flat on the back in a completely relaxed position. It allows the body to rest deeply while the mind remains aware. This meditation helps release physical and mental tension. It calms the nervous system and reduces stress. Practicing regularly improves sleep quality and relaxation. It is suitable for all age groups and fitness levels.

Benefits
  • Deep relaxation
  • Reduces stress
  • Improves sleep
  • Calms the mind
  • Relaxes the body

03

Yoga Nidra (Yogic Sleep)

Guided meditation for deep mental rest.

Yoga Nidra (Yogic Sleep)

Yoga Nidra is a guided lying meditation practiced in complete relaxation. It leads the mind into a deeply restful state between wakefulness and sleep. This practice helps release emotional and mental stress. It improves clarity, focus, and emotional balance. Regular practice restores energy and supports overall well-being. It is especially helpful for anxiety and fatigue relief.

Benefits
  • Deep mental relaxation
  • Reduces anxiety
  • Improves emotional balance
  • Restores energy
  • Enhances clarity

Standing Meditation

Tadasana Meditation (Mountain Pose)

Grounding meditation practiced in still standing.

Tadasana Meditation (Mountain Pose)

Tadasana Meditation is a standing practice that emphasizes stillness and awareness. It helps build a strong connection between the body and the ground. This meditation improves posture and balance while calming the mind. Standing tall encourages steady breathing and mental clarity. It develops patience and inner stability. Regular practice enhances grounding and presence in daily life.

Benefits
  • Improves grounding
  • Enhances posture
  • Builds balance
  • Calms the mind
  • Increases body awareness

Standing Breath Awareness

Mindful breathing practiced in a standing posture.

Standing Breath Awareness

Standing Breath Awareness focuses on observing the natural rhythm of the breath while standing still. It encourages calm attention without physical strain. This practice improves lung awareness and posture alignment. It helps reduce mental distractions and anxiety. Standing breathing meditation promotes stability and alertness. Regular practice improves focus and emotional control.

Benefits
  • Enhances breathing awareness
  • Improves focus
  • Reduces anxiety
  • Encourages calmness
  • Builds mental clarity

02

Tree Pose Stillness (Vrikshasana)

Balance-based standing meditation practice.

Tree Pose Stillness (Vrikshasana)

Tree Pose Stillness is a standing meditation that combines balance and focused awareness. It trains the mind to remain calm while the body maintains stability. This practice improves coordination and concentration. It encourages steady breathing and patience. Holding still builds inner strength and confidence. Regular practice enhances balance in both body and mind.

Benefits
  • Improves balance
  • Builds concentration
  • Strengthens legs
  • Enhances patience
  • Encourages mental calm
